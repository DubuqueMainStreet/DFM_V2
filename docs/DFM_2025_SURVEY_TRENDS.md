# Dubuque Farmers' Market 2025 — Survey Trends Summary  
**For vendor presentation**

Based on **357 public feedback responses** and **16 vendor survey responses** (2025).

---

## 1. Headline numbers

| Audience | Response count | Key takeaway |
|----------|----------------|--------------|
| **Public** | 357 | Strong attendance (65% at least monthly); satisfaction and atmosphere both rate ~4/5. |
| **Vendors** | 16 | Mixed sales: 38% below expectations, 38% as expected, 25% above; 38% said sales declined vs 2024. |

---

## 2. Biggest trends (aligned: public + vendors)

### A. **Comfort & amenities (seating, shade, restrooms)**  
- **Public:** **52%** want more seating; **48%** want improved restrooms; **39%** want better shade/rain protection.  
- **Vendors:** **38%** support permanent shade structures/tents.  
- **Takeaway:** Seating, restrooms, and shade are top physical improvements for shoppers and are supported by vendors.

### B. **Wayfinding & layout**  
- **Public:** “Easy to find vendors and products” and “comfortable places to sit” are top feature priorities; **31%** want wider aisles; **20%** want easier signage/maps. **33%** describe the market as “too crowded”; **18%** “chaotic”; **16%** “hard to navigate.”  
- **Vendors:** **31%** support wayfinding (signage, digital or printed maps); several mentioned spreading same product types and onsite maps.  
- **Takeaway:** Shoppers and vendors both want clearer layout, signage, and maps to reduce crowding and improve flow.

### C. **Marketing & visibility**  
- **Public:** Open-ended comments ask for “more marketing” and “promoting more buying for local vendors.”  
- **Vendors:** **50%** support increased marketing, vendor spotlights, and short-form social reels; **69%** would allow DMS to feature their business in social/storytelling.  
- **Takeaway:** Strong appetite for more marketing and vendor spotlights; most vendors are willing to be featured.

### D. **Dogs / pets**  
- **Public:** “Too many dogs” and “animals” appear in reasons for not attending; open-ended: “No dogs,” “No pets,” “Dogs ruin it,” “organization by vendor type [and] no longer permit dogs.”  
- **Vendors:** At least one noted: “Dogs are a huge distraction and issue! Many customers tell me…”  
- **Takeaway:** Recurring theme in both surveys; worth addressing in policy or communication.

### E. **Competition & product mix**  
- **Vendors:** **38%** said “competition / too many similar vendors” limited sales; comments on “overabundance of vendors selling beverages” and “distribution of vendors [and] same type of products throughout.”  
- **Public:** Some want “more options” and “fewer booths with same products back to back”; others want “focus on the vendors you have—promoting more buying for local.”  
- **Takeaway:** Vendors feel crowded by similar products; public wants either more variety or better promotion of existing vendors.

---

## 3. Public-only highlights

- **Attendance:** 34% once or twice a month, 31% every week or almost every week, 27% a few times a season.  
- **Satisfaction (1–5):** 47% gave 4, 24% gave 3, 23% gave 5 → average ~3.9.  
- **Atmosphere (1–5):** 47% gave 4, 31% gave 5 → average ~4.0.  
- **What they love (open text):** Fresh produce, variety of vendors, variety in general.  
- **Atmosphere words (multi-select):** Family-friendly (58%), Welcoming (52%), Busy (47%), Fun (43%), Community-focused (42%) — but also Too crowded (33%), Chaotic (18%), Hard to navigate (16%).  
- **Event programming:** Seasonal themed events (e.g. fall harvest) and live music are top; cooking demos and kids activities also popular.  
- **Products:** Fresh produce dominates; also baked goods, meat/poultry/eggs, flowers/plants, coffee.  
- **Improvement priorities (multi-select):** More seating (52%), Improved restrooms (48%), Better shade (39%), Better parking (33%), Wider aisles (31%), Signage/maps (20%), Family/kid spaces (17%).

---

## 4. Vendor-only highlights

- **Sales vs expectations:** Below (38%), As expected (38%), Above (25%).  
- **Sales vs 2024:** Declined (38%), About the same (25%), Not in 2024 (19%), Improved (19%).  
- **What limited sales (multi-select):** Weather (62%), Competition / too many similar vendors (38%), Low foot traffic (19%), Booth location/layout (19%), Inconsistent customer flow (12%).  
- **Enhancements supported (multi-select):** Increased marketing / vendor spotlights / short-form reels (50% each), Permanent shade (38%), Wayfinding/signage/maps (31%), Pop-up events (19%), Parking & shuttle (19%), Custodial/trash/recycling (19%).  
- **Single best improvement (open):** Recurring themes — vendor spotlights, more foot traffic/customers, spread similar vendors/distribution, onsite maps, weather alternatives, October start time, dogs as an issue.  
- **Social/media:** 69% Yes, 25% Maybe, 6% No to being featured by DMS.

---

## 5. Suggested talking points for vendor presentation

1. **Listen to both sides:** Public wants comfort (seating, shade, restrooms) and easier navigation; vendors want better visibility (marketing, spotlights) and layout/wayfinding. Many of these support each other.  
2. **Amenities:** Plan to highlight any 2026 moves on seating, shade, and restrooms — these are the top requested physical improvements.  
3. **Wayfinding:** Share plans for signage and maps (digital or printed) and how you’re thinking about layout and flow.  
4. **Marketing & spotlights:** Emphasize commitment to marketing and vendor spotlights; note that most vendors are open to being featured.  
5. **Dogs/pets:** Acknowledge feedback from both shoppers and vendors and any steps (policy or communication) you’re considering.  
6. **Product mix & competition:** Acknowledge vendor concerns about similar vendors and layout; tie to layout/wayfinding and “focus on vendors you have” feedback from the public.  
7. **Weather & seasonality:** Vendors cited weather and late-season drop-off; mention any weather contingencies or programming (e.g. pop-ups, timing) if relevant.
